## Go-to-market thesis
Primary wedge: agencies and freelance developers serving lead-driven small businesses.
Secondary wedge: small businesses with urgent lead-response needs.
Geography: English-speaking buyers more likely to pay.
Avoid broad low-intent traffic from markets with weak payment intent.

## Why this wedge first
- Agencies/devs can install FormBeep repeatedly across clients.
- They already understand forms, scripts, integrations, and webhooks.
- Integration-led discovery is more concentrated than trying to broadly educate small businesses.
- Agency/social proof can later unlock direct SMB acquisition.

## Channel strategy
### 1. Integration SEO and marketplace distribution
Objective: capture high-intent traffic from users already inside a platform workflow.

Priority actions
- Expand public integration landing pages so the site reflects actual compatibility, not just Webflow + Framer.
- Publish/update pages for WordPress, Wix, Squarespace, Shopify, Carrd, Ghost, React, Vue, Next.js, Django, Jotform/Tally/webhook use cases.
- Ship the WordPress submission improvements and publish.
- Rework Framer approach from component to plugin/distribution path.

Core message
- Get WhatsApp alerts from [Platform] forms in minutes.
- No rebuild. No heavy automation stack. Just instant lead alerts.

### 2. Community-led acquisition
Objective: earn early trust in the exact communities where implementers hang out.

Target communities
- WordPress-focused Reddit/forums/FB groups
- Webflow community spaces
- Framer community spaces once plugin path is clearer
- Indie Hackers
- No-code communities
- Freelance web dev communities

Execution rule
- Provide help-first posts: how to get instant form alerts, how to reduce missed leads, implementation guides, tiny tutorials.
- Avoid generic “check my product” spam.

### 3. Founder-led direct outreach
Objective: convert warm implementers and service providers faster than waiting for SEO.

Target list
- small agencies
- freelance website developers
- Webflow implementers
- WordPress maintenance freelancers
- no-code builders serving service businesses

Offer angle
- Install FormBeep on your next client site in 2 minutes.
- Faster lead response for clients.
- Free trial / free tier first.
- Agency plan exists when they scale.

### 4. Conversion and trust improvements
Objective: improve site readiness before scaling traffic aggressively.

Priority upgrades
- Stronger proof that FormBeep works across many platforms.
- More testimonials and named use cases.
- Better screenshots / real WhatsApp examples.
- Fix every onboarding trust leak, including incorrect emails.
- Add platform badges and use-case sections for agencies, freelancers, local businesses.

### 5. Product-led expansion path
Objective: widen TAM and reduce geography/channel dependence.

Expansion hypothesis
- SMS notifications may improve appeal in the US and for buyers who do not rely on WhatsApp.
- Keep as a tracked hypothesis, but do not let it delay current WhatsApp wedge unless direct demand keeps recurring.

## 30-day execution plan
### Days 1-7
- Audit analytics and establish funnel baseline.
- Publish/expand integration pages so the public site matches true support breadth.
- Prioritize WordPress publish path.
- Create outreach list of agencies/freelancers.
- Improve homepage and pricing copy for trust + agency use cases.

### Days 8-14
- Start posting useful integration content in targeted communities.
- Do direct outreach to agencies/freelancers.
- Add at least 2-3 proof assets: testimonial, example setup, screenshot, or short demo.
- Track traffic and CTA clicks by page.

### Days 15-30
- Double down on channels that generate qualified traffic/signups.
- Publish more integration/programmatic pages.
- Tighten onboarding using observed friction.
- Evaluate if SMS should move from idea to roadmap item based on new demand signals.

## Biggest risks
- Low urgency: people like the product but do not need it badly enough to pay
- Weak trust: buyers hesitate because this is a tiny/new tool
- Marketplace mismatch: integrations exist in docs but not in public acquisition pages
- Geo mismatch: traffic from countries with low payment intent dilutes metrics
- Channel over-expansion: too many channels at once

## Current recommendation
Be honest but aggressive:
- Do not pour broad traffic into the site yet.
- First strengthen trust + integration coverage + WordPress path.
- Then push targeted traffic from agency/dev communities and integration-led SEO.
