# FormBeep Content Analysis + 30-Day Calendar

Owner: Rumi (Blog & Content)
Date: 2026-03-31
Scope: competitor blog analysis + 30-day content calendar + 4 post outlines

## Executive takeaways
- Competitors are winning with **commercial-intent SEO**: comparisons, pricing pages, “free plan limits,” and setup guides.
- **Web2Phone** is the clearest content benchmark: it publishes a blog that maps directly to buying intent and product objections.
- **BeepMate** leans more on homepage/pricing/FAQ/code examples than a visible blog engine, which creates a gap FormBeep can exploit with better informational + comparison coverage.
- FormBeep’s best wedge is **US-first, SMS-first, multi-channel lead alerts** with platform-specific pages for **WordPress, Webflow, and Framer**.
- The content plan should move in this order: **problem → platform how-to → competitor comparison → pricing/alternatives → trust/reliability → distribution repurposing**.

---

## 1) Competitor blog analysis

### Web2Phone: the strongest SEO/content benchmark
What they appear to be doing well:
- Publishing **high-intent comparison posts**.
  - Example patterns already observed:
    - “Formspree vs Web2Phone (2026 Comparison)”
    - “Formspree Pricing Explained (2026 Breakdown)”
    - “Formspree Free Plan Limits and Pricing in 2026”
    - “How to Handle HTML Forms Without a Backend (2026 Guide)”
- Using blogs to attack the exact questions buyers ask before switching.
- Pairing blog content with **docs/integration pages**, which supports topical authority.
- Framing the problem sharply: **forms get buried in email**.

What this means for FormBeep:
- FormBeep should not rely only on generic educational posts.
- We need **comparison pages** and **platform-specific money pages** that match search intent.
- We should build an owned content cluster around:
  - `form-to-sms`
  - `form-to-whatsapp`
  - `form alerts`
  - `WordPress form notifications`
  - `Webflow/Framer notifications`
  - `Zapier/Make/Formspree alternatives`

### BeepMate: lighter content footprint, stronger product-led messaging
What they appear to be doing well:
- Homepage is a tight pitch: **Email to WhatsApp**.
- Pricing is simple and easy to understand.
- FAQ + code examples help reduce implementation friction.
- Their content surface feels more **product-led** than **blog-led**.

What this means for FormBeep:
- There is room to win with a more complete blog/content engine.
- FormBeep can out-position them on:
  - **SMS-first alerts**
  - **multi-channel fallback**
  - **routing rules**
  - **WordPress trust/installability**
  - **small-team reliability**

### Content gap summary
Competitors are mostly answering:
- What is this?
- How much does it cost?
- How do I compare it to X?
- Can I set it up quickly?

FormBeep should add:
- **Which channel should I use first?** (SMS vs WhatsApp vs email)
- **How do I avoid missing leads?**
- **How do I route alerts by form/page/team?**
- **What is the best setup for WordPress/Webflow/Framer?**
- **What should I switch from Zapier/Make/Formspree to FormBeep?**

Competitors are proving the market wants a simple answer to one question:
**“How do I make sure a form lead reaches me instantly?”**

**“Send it to SMS first, back it up with WhatsApp and email, and route it to the right person automatically.”**
