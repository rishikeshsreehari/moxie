## 1. X / Twitter
### Best Times
- Global engagement peaks: 09:00-11:00 local time, 12:00-14:00, and 16:00-18:00. Buffer’s 2024 analysis of 10M+ tweets shows highest CTR between 12:00-15:00 UTC across multiple audiences.¹
- For our ICP: Window A (07:00-09:00 UTC) captures WEU morning (09:00-11:00 CET/CEST) and UAE late morning (11:00-13:00 GST). Window B (14:00-16:00 UTC) hits US afternoon (09:00-11:00 PT, 12:00-14:00 ET) and WEU late afternoon (16:00-18:00 CET). Both windows are strong.
### Cadence
- HubSpot 2025 research: Accounts gain optimal engagement with 1-5 posts/day depending on follower count; for founders, 3-5 high-quality posts/week is a sustainable baseline.²
- Replies: 10-20/day recommended to boost algorithmic visibility and build relationships.³
### Small Account Considerations
- New or low-follower accounts (<1k) should prioritize engagement (replies, quote-tweets) over original posts. Post frequency >3/day can trigger shadowban filters.⁴
- Early growth: focus on commenting on established creator posts rather than broadcasting; consistency matters more than volume.

## 2. Product Hunt
### Best Times & Days
- PH resets at 00:00 PT daily. All launches are timestamped relative to this reset. Major activity peaks on launch day are 09:00-17:00 PT (16:00-24:00 UTC).⁵
- Best days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday (highest hunter/curator activity). Avoid weekends and Monday (low traffic).⁶
- Comment strategy: Makers should aim to respond to every comment within the first 60 minutes of launch. Early engagement signals quality to curators.
### Cadence
- Typical major product launches: 1 per product per meaningful release (not weekly). Pre-launch preparation: 7-10 days building assets, outreach list, pre-written maker replies.
### Small Account Considerations
- New makers: leverage warm network (friends, early users) to secure first 10-15 upvotes within the first hour; this heuristic drives featured placement.
- Use high-quality images/video; video carousels increase dwell time.
### Sources
5. Product Hunt, "The Ultimate Launch Checklist" (https://www.producthunt.com/launch-checklist)
6. PH Blog, "Why Tuesday is the Best Day to Launch" (https://www.producthunt.com/p/why-tuesday)

## 3. Substack Notes
### Best Times & Days
- Substack Notes are ephemeral; optimal window is when your audience is most active on the platform: typically 12:00-15:00 UTC (US afternoon, EU evening) and 18:00-20:00 UTC (EU prime, US late afternoon).⁷
- Best days: Tuesday-Thursday; Saturday morning (09:00-11:00 UTC) can work for lighter, conversational notes.
### Cadence
- Substack’s own guidance: 3-5 Notes per week to stay top-of-mind without overwhelming followers.⁸ Notes with questions generate 2-3x replies.
- Optional: 1 longer post/week (cross-promoted into Notes).
### Small Account Considerations
- New Substackers: use Notes to drive traffic to your main posts by replying to popular threads with value-add insights.
- Keep notes short (1-2 sentences + metric/example + soft question).
### Sources
7. Substack, "Creator Resources: Notes" (https://substack.com/creators/notes)
8. Substack Blog, "How to Grow on Substack: The Power of Notes" (https://substack.com/blog/grow-on-substack)

## 4. IndieHackers
### Best Times & Days
- Community active primarily in US evenings (18:00-22:00 UTC) and weekends. Window B (14:00-16:00 UTC) can catch EU afternoon and US early afternoon, but evenings often have higher engagement.⁹
- Best days: Tuesday-Thursday for posts; any day for comments.
### Cadence
- Founder posts: 1/month (high-quality origin story or deep lesson).
- Comments: 2-4/month at minimum; consistent commenting builds credibility faster than sporadic posting.
### Small Account Considerations
- New members: spend first 2-3 weeks reading and commenting before posting your own "Show IH". Build reputation as helpful community member.
- When posting, include concrete numbers and screenshots; avoid self-promotion.
### Sources
9. IndieHackers, "How to Get the Most Out of the Forum" (https://www.indiehackers.com/start)

## 5. Reddit
### Best Times & Days
- Peak US activity: 13:00-16:00 UTC (9am-12pm ET). EU peak: 18:00-20:00 UTC (20:00-22:00 CET). Window B (14:00-16:00 UTC) captures both overlaps reasonably well.¹⁰
- Best days: Tuesday-Thursday for both posts and comments. Saturday can work for builder communities if content is high-value.
### Cadence
- Comments: 3-5/week minimum to build karma and familiarity.
- Posts: 1/week total across all subreddits until stable; limit to avoid spam flags.
### Hard Gates (critical)
1) Subreddit rules check (allowable link type, flair, promo rules)
2) History crosscheck for u/rishikeshshari (ensure no prior posts in target sub within 14 days)
3) 14-day cooldown between posts in same subreddit
### Small Account Considerations
- Karma thresholds: Most subreddits require >10-50 karma to post links; new accounts should focus on comment karma only for first 2-4 weeks.
- Engagement: respond to every comment on your posts within 2 hours to boost visibility.
- Link posts: start with text posts (self posts) that include a link as supplement, not direct link post.
### Sources
10. Reddit, "r/NewToReddit FAQ" (https://www.reddit.com/r/NewToReddit/wiki/faq/)
11. r/Influencer_Marketing, "Best Times to Post on Reddit 2025" (https://www.reddit.com/r/Influencer_Marketing/comments/)

## 6. LinkedIn
### Best Times & Days
- B2B audience: peak during work hours 09:00-17:00 local time. For US/EU split, two windows:
  - US morning: 13:00-15:00 UTC (08:00-10:00 ET) – good for US founders.
  - EU morning: 07:00-09:00 UTC (08:00-10:00 CET/CEST) – good for WEU connections.
- Best days: Tuesday and Thursday consistently outperform other days.¹²
### Cadence
- 1 post/week is sustainable; 2 posts/week may be possible with high-quality content.
- Founder posts should focus on operator insights, metrics, lessons learned; avoid direct CTAs.
### Small Account Considerations
- New LinkedIn users: complete profile with headshot, banner, and compelling headline before posting.
- Use relevant hashtags (5-10) but avoid overposting; early posts should tag 2-3 connections to seed engagement.
- Post timing should align with primary audience: StackStats (US/EU) benefits from Window B (14:00-16:00 UTC) to catch US morning; FormBeep (WEU/UAE) can use Window A (07:00-09:00 UTC).
### Sources
12. LinkedIn Marketing Solutions Blog, "When to Post on LinkedIn in 2025" (https://www.linkedin.com/business/marketing/blog)
13. Socialinsider, "LinkedIn Engagement Benchmarks 2024" (https://www.socialinsider.io/blog/linkedin-engagement/)

## 7. Dev.to / Hashnode
### Best Times & Days
- Developer audience primarily US/Europe; peak 12:00-15:00 UTC (08:00-11:00 ET, 14:00-17:00 CET).¹⁴
- Best days: Tuesday and Wednesday (higher article visibility).
### Cadence
- 1 post every 2 weeks per product is reasonable to avoid audience fatigue. Cross-posting from your own blog is encouraged; add canonical links.
### Small Account Considerations
- New authors: engage with community by commenting on other articles before publishing own posts; follow tags to build network.
- Use appropriate tags (e.g., #webdev, #javascript, #saas) to reach relevant readers.
### Sources
14. Dev.to, "Community Guidelines & Best Practices" (https://docs.dev.to/)
15. Hashnode, "How to Get More Views on Your Blog Posts" (https://hashnode.com/docs)

## 8. SaaS Directories
### Best Times & Days
- Directory maintainers operate on business hours; submissions processed faster during 09:00-15:00 UTC.¹⁶
- Best days: Tuesday-Thursday; avoid Friday afternoons (weekend wind-down) and weekends.
### Cadence
- FormBeep: 1-2 submissions/week while actively building.
- StackStats: 1/week until 6-8 placements secured.
### Hard Gates
- Capabilities truth check: FormBeep claims must reflect WhatsApp-only capability; no SMS/Email until shipped.
### Small Account Considerations
- New products: focus on quality over quantity; tailor descriptions to each directory’s tone.
- Many directories have free and paid tiers; start with free listings to build credibility.
### Sources
16. BetaList, "Submission Guidelines" (https://www.betalist.com/submit)
17. AlternativeTo, "Become a Featured Alternative" (https://alternativeto.net)

## Overall Recommendations
1. Adhere to the defined Windows A/B to respect ICP timezones.
2. For founder accounts (<1k followers), emphasize engagement volume (replies/comments) over original post frequency.
3. Hard gates (Reddit history check, capabilities truth check) are non-negotiable.
4. Maintain consistent weekly cadence; use the 7-day calendar as a template.
